IO Testing — More States for Failed Items

A feedback loop so the field can tell which failed IOs are ready to re-check, and which are waiting on engineering.

Draft for review · taskboard #2233 · prepared by Nika

The problem. Today an IO is Not Tested, Passed, or Failed. A failed item just sits there — there's no way to tell which failures have since been sorted out (part arrived, wiring redone) and are ready to test again, versus which are still genuinely stuck. With one verifier walking 144 IOs — and far more once 5 MCMs are commissioned in parallel — someone ends up dedicated full-time to re-walking the failed list. We need a couple more states so the failed items carry their own status.

The statuses

An IO's status is freely changeable between:

Not Tested   Failed   Addressed   Passed

Clarification is not a separate status. The item stays Failed the whole time — it just carries a tag: first "needs clarification" (the engineer's queue), then "Clarification Added" once the engineer has answered. That way electricians only ever filter for one thing — Failed — and never miss an item.

  • UP already exists — everything the tablet does pushes to the cloud exactly like today.
  • DOWN is the only new plumbing, and it's deliberately narrow: just the engineer's clarification answer + the "Clarification Added" tag coming back to the tablet. Nothing else needs to come down.
  • It must survive the tablet being offline for days and land when it reconnects — that's the whole point of the feature.
  • If the tablet already moved the item on (e.g. electrician marked it Addressed) while the engineer was typing in the cloud, the tablet wins — the cloud never stomps the field. This keeps us clear of the data-loss problems we've hit before.
One thing to confirm before build. This whole design assumes the engineer adds clarification on the cloud, which is why we need the DOWN sync. If your engineers actually work onsite in the tablet too, then nothing needs to come back down and the feature shrinks to just the four statuses + the tag — much simpler and lower risk.
